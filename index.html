Featured Project

Housing Rent Analysis Tool

This project aims to develop a comprehensive tool for analyzing 'fair market rates' (FMR) in housing rents at the metropolitan area level and understanding their relationship with economic indicators such as the consumer price index (CPI). Leveraging advanced data analytics and visualization techniques, it provides valuable insights into government housing programs.

Undertaken as part of the 'Programming and Quality in Analytics' course, this project showcases my proficiency in Python, with a strong emphasis on programming quality and best practices. While some aspects of the analysis could benefit from time-series techniques, the focus here is on core Python libraries, including Pandas and NumPy.

In this project, I fetched data from five different sources, processed it into a usable format, and gained experience working with APIs. A key aspect was ensuring code reusability, making the project modular and adaptable. The tool can fetch data for any metropolitan area and perform analyses, updating with the latest available data for accurate insights.

The project tests two primary hypotheses:
Hypothesis 1: There is a correlation between FMR and CPI, where CPI influences FMR with a time lag.
Hypothesis 2: Anomalies in FMR prices can be detected through the study of FMR and CPI, indicating regulatory interventions in housing rents.
